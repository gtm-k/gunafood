# GunaFood — Ayurvedic Food Wisdom in Your Pocket

**Classify any food as Sattvic, Rajasic, or Tamasic using the ancient Ayurvedic Guna framework.**

GunaFood is a mobile app that brings 5,000 years of Ayurvedic food wisdom to your fingertips. Search any food, understand its effect on your mind, body, and spirit, and learn the best time to eat it — all rooted in the Bhagavad Gita, Charaka Samhita, and classical Ayurvedic texts.

---

## The Three Gunas

In the ancient Ayurvedic framework, all food carries one of three fundamental qualities:

- **Sattvic** (Purity & Clarity) — Fresh, natural foods that promote mental clarity, calm, and spiritual growth. *Examples: ghee, fresh fruits, rice, moong dal, honey.*

- **Rajasic** (Energy & Drive) — Stimulating foods that fuel action and ambition but can create restlessness in excess. *Examples: coffee, chili, garlic, onion, fried foods.*

- **Tamasic** (Rest & Grounding) — Heavy, processed, or stale foods that promote inertia and heaviness. *Examples: leftovers, alcohol, processed foods.*

## Features

### Search & Classify
Type any food name and instantly see its Guna classification with:
- Ayurvedic rationale explaining *why* it's classified this way
- Source citations from real classical texts
- "Best time to eat" and "Avoid when" contextual guidance
- How preparation method changes the classification

### "What Should I Eat Now?"
Get personalized food suggestions based on:
- Time of day (morning, midday, evening, night)
- Your current context (before meditation, after workout, during stress)
- Ayurvedic seasonal wisdom (6 seasons of Ritucharya)

### Learn the Three Gunas
Beautiful, accessible educational content explaining each Guna's effects on mind, body, and spirit — with quotes from the Bhagavad Gita and Charaka Samhita.

### Discover Your Guna Tendency
A gentle 3-question quiz helps you understand your dominant Guna tendency, personalizing your experience.

## Philosophy

*"Your food is your consciousness."*

GunaFood is built on the belief that what we eat shapes not just our bodies, but our minds and spirits. The ancient Ayurvedic framework of the three Gunas offers a time-tested lens for understanding food beyond calories and macros — as a force that can promote clarity, energy, or heaviness in our daily lives.

We present this wisdom with warmth, honesty, and respect for the tradition. Where classical texts disagree, we show the nuance. Where modern understanding differs from ancient classification, we acknowledge both perspectives. GunaFood is a guide, not a doctrine.
